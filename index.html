I am currently a master of chemical engineering at Sungkyunkwan University, and my main research direction is AI analysis of nanosensor signals. My research goal is to improve the stability and sensitivity of sensors through the combination of AI and nanosensors, so that they can be applied in various fields such as healthcare, environmental monitoring, and food safety. Through my research, I aim to develop new methods and algorithms for the analysis of nanosensor signals using AI. By optimizing the performance of nanosensors through AI analysis, I hope to contribute to the development of more accurate, reliable, and cost-effective sensor systems. Ultimately, my goal is to create innovative sensor technologies that can be used to address critical challenges facing our society today.

Before I joined Sungkyunkwan University, I was studying at QUST of Mechanical Engineering, and got an exchange opportunity to Hanyang University after two years with excellent results. Afterwards, I obtained a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from Hanyang University. Now I am a researcher in cholab at skku, worked with Prof. Sooyeon Cho.And through hard work, in February 2024, I will go to the Lawrence Berkeley Laboratory (LBNL) Electronic Materials Research Center for exchange.
